Why Most Operators Choose Professional Hosting

Running an S21 Pro at home sounds simple until you check what it actually needs. This machine pulls 3,510W continuously. That means a dedicated 20-amp 240V circuit at minimum. It also runs at 75 decibels, which is louder than most vacuum cleaners. And it throws serious heat into whatever room it sits in.

Professional hosting at $0.07/kWh fixed solves all of that. You get commercial power pricing, 24/7 uptime monitoring, proper cooling infrastructure, and no noise or heat in your space. You pay a management layer for that. At current hashprice, the numbers still work in your favour every month.

FAQ

Common Questions About the S21 Pro

What is the Antminer S21 Pro hashrate?

The S21 Pro runs at 234TH/s on the SHA-256 algorithm. That makes it one of the highest-output air-cooled Bitcoin miners in the S21 lineup, alongside the S21 XP at 270TH/s.

Is the Antminer S21 Pro profitable in 2026?

At $0.07/kWh the S21 Pro nets roughly $3.04 per day based on current hashprice of $38.20/PH. At residential rates of $0.12/kWh or higher it runs at a loss. Power cost is the deciding factor.

Can I run the S21 Pro at home?

Technically yes, but it is rarely practical. The machine needs a dedicated 20-amp 240V circuit, runs at 75 dB, and generates continuous heat equivalent to three full-size space heaters. Most operators use professional hosting instead.

How does the S21 Pro compare to the S21 XP?

The XP runs at 270TH/s with 13.5 J/TH efficiency. Both numbers beat the Pro. But the XP costs more upfront. The Pro is the better value entry point if you are not maxing out efficiency requirements.

What hosting rate do I need to profit with the S21 Pro?

You need electricity below $0.10/kWh to run profitably at current difficulty and hashprice. The sweet spot is $0.07/kWh or lower, which is what professional US hosting facilities typically offer on fixed contracts.

Does the S21 Pro come with a power supply?

Yes. The S21 Pro has a built-in PSU. You do not need to purchase a separate power supply unit, which simplifies setup compared to older Bitmain models that required external PSUs.
